QR Code Security Best Practices

  • Verify the Source: Only scan QR codes from trusted sources, as malicious codes can redirect you to phishing sites or download malware.
  • Use a QR Code Scanner with Security Features: Choose scanners that allow you to preview the URL before opening it to avoid malicious content.
  • Avoid Scanning Random Codes: Be cautious when scanning QR codes from public places, advertisements, or unknown locations, as they may lead to security risks.
  • Update Your Device and Apps: Keep your device’s software and QR code scanning apps up-to-date to prevent vulnerabilities from being exploited.
  • Watch Out for QR Code Overlays: Criminals can place fake QR codes over legitimate ones, so inspect codes in public places carefully.
  • Don’t Share Sensitive Information: Avoid entering sensitive personal or financial information after scanning a QR code, especially if the destination website seems suspicious.
  • Use Security Software: Make sure you have security software that can detect malicious links and prevent harmful actions after scanning a QR code.
  • Monitor Financial Transactions: If you use QR codes for financial transactions, ensure the website is secure and always double-check the transaction details before proceeding.
